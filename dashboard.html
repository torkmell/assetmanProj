    How a Trade Gets Made — Decision Flow

    STEP 1 · LAST DAY OF MONTH
    Rank sectors. Compute 12m skip-1m return, z-score cross-sectionally. Identify top-quartile basket (typically 7–8 industries).
    STEP 2 · LAST DAY OF MONTH
    Read the regime. Compute the macro composite from prior-month-end data. Map to gross exposure in [0.3, 1.0].
    STEP 3 · OVERNIGHT
    Generate orders. Equal-weight target weights × gross exposure. Compute delta vs current book. Stagger over 2 trading days at <5% ADV per name.
    STEP 4 · DAILY
    Monitor. Drawdown vs trigger, factor exposure tracking, individual position limit checks. No discretionary intramonth rebalances.

    Factor Regression — Alpha vs FF5 + MOM

    OLS with Newey-West (HAC) standard errors. The strategy is, honestly, repackaged factor beta — there is no statistically significant alpha after controlling for market, size, value, profitability, investment, and momentum factors. We deliver a better risk-adjusted combination of these exposures, not a magic signal.

    Deflated Sharpe Ratio (Bailey & López de Prado)

    Adjusts the observed Sharpe for the number of trials and the non-normality of returns. Honest disclosure: a Sharpe of 0.56 does not survive multiple-testing correction at the 12-trial parameter grid. We treat this as a brake on overclaiming, not as a disqualifier — the macro overlay is a single, ex-ante hypothesis, not data-mined.
