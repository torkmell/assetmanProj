A systematic, macro-conditioned sector rotation fund that captures the lag between macro-regime shifts and institutional sector re-allocation, expressed through liquid US equity sector ETFs.

The Edge

What inefficiency we exploit. Sector returns persistently underreact to macro regime transitions. Institutional benchmark-driven flows rebalance sector exposures with a multi-month lag, leaving a window in which a systematic momentum signal — conditioned on the prevailing macro regime — captures the dislocation before it closes.
Why it persists. Three structural reasons: (1) benchmark mandates discourage rapid sector tilts; (2) macro signals are noisy and slow to converge on consensus; (3) the strategy is capacity-constrained at the institutional scale that would otherwise arbitrage it. Crowding risk is real but bounded by the same factors that create the edge.

How It Works — Three Signal Components

1Cross-Sectional Momentum
12-month total return, skip-1-month, z-scored across the universe each month-end. Long the top quartile of sectors.
2Macro Regime Composite
A four-factor risk-on/risk-off composite: credit spreads, real-rate trend, industrial production YoY, commodities momentum. All inputs lagged one month — no look-ahead.
3Risk-Aware Sizing
The macro composite scales total gross exposure between 30% (risk-off) and 100% (risk-on); uninvested capital earns the risk-free rate. Equal-weight within the long sleeve.

Universe & Instruments

Tradable universe. 11 SPDR US sector ETFs XLE · XLF · XLK · XLI · XLY · XLP · XLV · XLU · XLB · XLRE · XLC plus SHY as the cash-equivalent during risk-off periods. All instruments are deeply liquid; daily volumes range from hundreds of millions to several billion USD.
Backtest proxy. Fama–French 30-industry value-weighted portfolios (1990–2018, ~29 years). Avoids survivorship bias and ETF-launch dating issues; the live ETF universe is the institutional implementation of the same exposures.

Risk Framework

  • Gross exposure cap: 100% (long-only fund); no leverage.
  • Per-sector position limit: 20% NAV.
  • Vol target: 10–12% annualised, achieved via regime-scaled gross exposure rather than leverage.
  • Drawdown trigger: de-risk to 50% gross at –12% peak-to-trough; halt new risk at –18%.
  • Factor monitoring: monthly regression of returns vs Fama–French 5 + MOM; flag if factor beta exceeds tolerance.
  • Stress testing: 2000 dot-com, 2008 GFC, 2011 Eurozone, 2015 China, 2018 vol spike.

Why Now

Macro regime variance is at multi-decade highs (rates, inflation, growth all in active transitions), and sector dispersion within US equities has widened — both conditions historically associated with outperformance of systematic sector-rotation strategies. Concurrent strategies in this space are predominantly discretionary or single-signal; the differentiated combination of cross-sectional momentum with a transparent macro overlay is, to our knowledge, not crowded.
